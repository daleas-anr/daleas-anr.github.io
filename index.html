DALEAS

Détection Automatique à Large Échelle d'Anomalies Spatiales — Large-Scale Automatic Detection of Spatial Anomalies

A scalable, multimodal tool to spot anomalies in large streaming data — from disinformation to digital twins and the sky.

At a glance

How. DALEAS is a French 3-year project funded by the French National Research Agency (ANR), started in January 2026.

What. The DALEAS project develops a proof of concept for a multimodal tool that detects anomalies in large-scale streaming data — anything that does not match a trusted geospatial reference, from a fake-news post to an out-of-date digital twin. Its core is honest geospatial indexing: every source is anchored in space and kept with the uncertainty it really carries — never collapsed to a single point — so it can be confronted, at scale, with certified references such as IGN's LiDAR HD. Inspired by the astronomical alert system Fink and built on Apache Spark, the system is scalable by design.

Who. DALEAS brings together partners specialized in Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence (LASTIG, CNRS), as well as Media (France Télévisions) and a security & cyber-defence partner.

Objectives

DALEAS aims to prove that a single, generic infrastructure can serve very different fields from one shared core, letting each user focus on their own question while the infrastructure handles the computing. Its genericity is validated by applying the same building blocks to three deliberately different use cases:

  • Combating disinformation — checking whether a video really shows the place it claims, by confronting it with certified geospatial references (e.g. IGN's LiDAR HD).
  • Updating spatial datasets — flagging inconsistencies in digital twins of the territory that signal an out-of-date map.
  • Detecting artificial satellites — spotting anomalies in large astronomical data streams.

DALEAS is committed to open, documented components following the FAIR4RS principles — transparency, adaptability, reproducibility and long-term usability of the research software.
