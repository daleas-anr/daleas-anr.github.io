A scalable, multimodal tool to spot anomalies in large streaming data — from disinformation to digital twins and the sky.

At a glance

How. DALEAS is a French 3-year project funded by the French National Research Agency (ANR), started in January 2026.

What. The DALEAS project aims to develop a proof of concept for a multimodal tool capable of processing large-scale streaming multimedia data across various domains such as disinformation, cartography, cybersecurity, and space exploration. Inspired by the astronomical alert system Fink, the architecture will enable diverse users (journalists, scientists, and other professionals) to analyze data by comparing it with verified reference sources. Built on Apache Spark, the system will be scalable by design. Its core function is anomaly detection, such as identifying fake news or inconsistencies in digital twins.

Who. DALEAS brings together partners specialized in Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence (LASTIG, CNRS), as well as Media (France Télévisions) and a security & cyber-defence partner.

Objectives

DALEAS designs a scalable, reusable infrastructure to analyse massive streams of multimedia data (images, video, text, 3D point clouds) by confronting them with trusted reference corpora, in order to surface anomalies — what does not match the reference. The architecture is inspired by Fink, an alert system originally built for astronomy, and is implemented on Apache Spark so it scales by design across a cluster or the cloud.

The proof of concept is designed, evaluated and exploited through three use cases:

  • Combating disinformation — detecting fake or manipulated content against verified, certified references.
  • Updating spatial datasets — flagging inconsistencies in digital twins of the territory that signal an out-of-date map.
  • Detecting artificial satellites — spotting anomalies in large astronomical data streams.

DALEAS is committed to open, documented components following the FAIR4RS principles — transparency, adaptability, reproducibility and long-term usability of the research software.
